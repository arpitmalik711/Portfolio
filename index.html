01
Upper A-Arm Bending (BAJA 2026)
Baja ATV — Suspension and Traction Test
📐 What I Planned

Initial suspension geometry designed for 21 in AFCO shocks. FEA used to validate arm strength and mounting loads.

💥 Went Wrong

Afco OIL leak issue during Testing phase.

💥 Replacement

Replaced with fox(16in) → Damper Pivot shifted towards centre of arm + bracket length increased → moment increased → bending stress increased

⚙️ What Happened

During suspension & traction test, high obstacle impact caused upper A-arm pipes to bend due to increased bending moment.

✅ What I Did

Reinforced arm by welding an additional primary pipe (29.2 mm dia) to both members to increase bending stiffness. Fix performed on-site during competition.

💡

What I Learned Design changes without re-validation can lead to unexpected failure. Always re-check load path, bending moment, and stiffness after geometry changes.

02
Chassis Member bending (BAJA 2025)
Baja ATV Chassis — Suspension Load Case
📐 What I Planned

The damper mount was designed to transfer suspension load directly into the chassis member. In CAD and FEA, the load was assumed to act near a supported region so that forces would flow through the chassis without creating excessive bending.

💥 What Went Wrong

During fabrication, the damper mount was welded away from of a single tubular member without additional support. This shifted the load application point away from a node and created a long unsupported span in the pipe.

⚙️ What Happened

During suspension testing, the damper force acted at the center of the tube, creating a high bending moment (M = F·L / 4). The pipe behaved like a simply supported beam with a point load at mid-span, causing tensile stress on one side and compressive stress on the other. The stress exceeded allowable limits, leading to local yielding, permanent bending, and risk of weld cracking due to stress concentration.

✅ What I Did

To improve load transfer, an additional pipe was welded at the damper mounting location to create a proper load path and increase local stiffness. This reduced the effective span, increased moment of inertia, and introduced triangulation. The load was redistributed into multiple members, converting the loading condition from bending-dominant to axial-dominant and significantly reducing peak stress and deformation.

🖥️ Calculation & FEA PROOF

CLICK ANYWHERE on this card to view the 7-step FEA simulation of the chassis member bending failure analysis.

💡

What I Learned Incorrect load path and mid-span loading can create large bending moments in chassis members. Suspension mounts should always be connected near nodes or triangulated members to avoid bending-dominated loading. Small fabrication changes can drastically affect stiffness, stress distribution, and fatigue life, so load path validation must be done before welding.

03
The Bend That Shouldn't Be Welded
Chassis Fabrication — E-Moped Frame
📐 What I Planned

The main chassis pipe required bending at a specific angle and radius while still carrying most of the structural load. Proper pipe bending was required to maintain strength and geometry.

💥 Went Wrong

Due to lack of bending machine, the pipe was cut from the inner side, manually bent to shape, and then welded. This shortcut helped fabrication, but introduced a welded joint exactly at the high-stress bend region.

⚙️ What Happened

The cut-and-weld method disturbed the original cross-section continuity. The neutral axis shifted inward and higher tensile stress developed at the outer fiber of the bend. The weld zone created stress concentration, reducing fatigue strength of the load-carrying member.

✅ What I Did

I arranged proper pipe bending using a bending machine to maintain uniform section, correct bend radius, and continuous grain flow. This improved structural reliability and increased fatigue life of the chassis member.

💡

What I Learned In load-carrying structures, fabrication shortcuts can reduce strength even if geometry looks correct. Maintaining section continuity during bending is critical to avoid stress concentration and fatigue failure. Manufacturing method is part of design, not a separate step.
